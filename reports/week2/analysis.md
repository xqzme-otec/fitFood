## Learning points
*During the week, our team learned how to evaluate the product from the perspective of potential users, paying special attention to solving real customer problems. We realized that prioritization is an essential skill. There were disagreements during the team discussions, as some of the desired features were technically difficult to implement. However, the process of writing user stories and prioritizing has taught us to objectively evaluate our resources and technical limitations. In addition, a joint call with the customer was invaluable. This helped us understand their real needs, refine our initial analysis, and identify a clear strategy moving forward.
## Validated assumptions
*Rejected: Technical impossibility: Initially, we assumed that implementing synonym recognition would be too technically difficult and practically impossible for our timeline. However, during the meeting, the customer explained how important this feature is for user convenience. More importantly, the customer offered a practical solution. This refuted our assumption that this was impossible, proving that the task was completely solvable and justifying an increase in its priority.

## Planned response
*Parallel research of synonyms:
*Following the customer's advice, we will appoint a team member to research and test the proposed open source synonyms dictionaries. If integration turns out to be simple, then we will consider the possibility of increasing its priority in the next development cycles. 
*First, we manually enter the products:
*Since scanning store receipts involves serious technical risks, the development of MVP version 1 will primarily focus on creating an ideal manual input interface. This ensures that users will always be able to add new products, acting as a reliable backup option during parallel scanner development.
*Scope protection area:
*Despite the discovery of new technical solutions, we will strictly adhere to our MVP v1 scope. We will focus our efforts on developing the main mandatory products: registration, adding products, recipe recommendations and macronutrient tracking to ensure that the finished product is received by the deadline.
